# BLACKOUT — Hackathon Theme

**Event:** GDGC UOA Hackathon 2026 (codename GRIDAKL)
**Time:** 28 hours

## Scenario

Solar flare hits Earth at 3:47am. Physical infrastructure intact. All digital records wiped simultaneously — banking, credentials, identities, property titles, medical records, contracts. 9 million people. Nobody knows how long this lasts.

## 8 Example Problem Areas

These are **starting points, not requirements**. Build something the brief didn't think of.

| # | Title | Core question |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 01 | Identity without records | How does someone prove who they are when every record is gone? |
| 02 | Ownership without proof | How do you establish/protect what's yours when documents don't exist? |
| 03 | Trust without authority | Banks, govts, courts frozen — how do communities enforce agreements? |
| 04 | Credentials without verification | Doctors/engineers can't prove skills — who do you trust with what? |
| 05 | Exchange without currency | Accounts frozen, cash scarce, digital payments down — how do people trade? |
| 06 | Decisions without governance | Collective decisions with no formal structure, no verifiable voter list |
| 07 | Information without truth | Rumours vs facts — how does a community surface reliable information? |
| 08 | Resources without coordination | Supply exists but isn't where it's needed — match it with no central coordinator |

## What to build

Something novel. Not a direct implementation of the 8 above. The brief says: "You might solve one, part of one, or **something we haven't thought of**."
